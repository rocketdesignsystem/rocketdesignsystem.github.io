Accessibility

At Coles, we believe that everyone is equal and that everyone has the right to be included. We are excited by human diversity and embrace the creativity it brings.

Accessibility is embedded into the delivery of our products and services end-to-end from design, to build, to test, to launch.

The Coles Design System helps ensure we create and maintain inclusive and accessible digital interactions. Components in this Design System are WCAG 2.1, AA compliant and its use will improve the accessibility of our digital solutions.

Coles' commitment to accessibility

At Coles, we know we are better when everyone is offered the same opportunities to perform at their best, to be happy, healthy and feel accepted for who they are.

We are committed to building safe and supportive environments for all, regardless of age, disability, race, gender identity, sexual orientation, ethnicity, or background.

Our Coles Accessibility Action Plan highlights the commitment Coles has made to improve the shopping experience of all Australians, and the working conditions of team members with a disability.

Our mission

To create and maintain digital content and interactions that is accessible to people with disabilities. To improve the accessibility of our stores, sites, and workplaces for people with disability, whilst supporting our customers and communities with sustainable and leading initiatives.

Actions & Behaviours

  • Adoption of WCAG 2.1 standards
  • Accessibility training to team members
  • Monitoring of Coles Digital Assets for Accessibility compliance
  • Accessibility evaluation and reports
  • Consultancy and engagement with people with disability
  • Participation in the Access & Inclusion Index (Australian Network on Disability)
  • Relationships with disability groups
  • Customer research and User testing with people with disabilities

Assistive Technology

Assistive technology describes tools used by people with disabilities to accomplish tasks. In the digital space, assistive technologies refer to software and hardware that people use to improve digital experiences. This includes screen readers that read aloud web pages for people who cannot read the text, screen magnifiers for people with some types of low vision, and voice recognition software and selection switches for people who cannot use a keyboard or mouse.

Examples of Assistive Technology

  • Screen readers (e.g. JAWS, VoiceOver)
  • Screen magnification software
  • Text readers
  • Speech input software
  • Alternative input devices:
    • Head pointers
    • Motion tracking or eye tracking
    • Single switch entry devices
